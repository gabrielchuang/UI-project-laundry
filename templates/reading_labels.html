Understanding Clothing Care Labels

Introduction to Clothing Labels

Usually, the labels on your clothes will tell you how to wash them. Understanding these symbols can help you properly care for your garments and make them last longer.

Let's explore the common symbols you'll find on clothing labels and what they mean.

Understanding the Symbols

The symbols on the labels can be confusing, but they are quite systematic! Each symbol represents a specific care instruction for your garment.

The chart below shows the most common laundry symbols you'll encounter:

Washing Symbols

This icon represents the drum of the washing machine, with water in it. The lines underneath represent the gentleness of the cycle. The icons inside represent the temperature of the water.

  • The washtub symbol indicates that the item can be machine washed
  • Temperature is shown by numbers (30°, 40°, etc.) or dots
  • Lines under the washtub indicate a gentle cycle is required
  • A cross through the symbol means do not wash

Drying Symbols

This square-and-circle symbol looks like a tumble dryer.

  • The dots inside represent heat levels (more dots = higher heat)
  • If there isn't a circle inside, it refers to drying in general (not just in a tumble dryer)
  • A cross through the symbol means do not tumble dry

Ironing Symbols

These are quite self-explanatory. Similar to wash and dry, the dots represent temperature.

  • One dot means low temperature (up to 110°C)
  • Two dots means medium temperature (up to 150°C)
  • Three dots means high temperature (up to 200°C)
  • A cross through the iron means do not iron

Bleaching Symbols

This is the most complex symbol set.

  • The triangle symbol represents bleach
  • There are two different types of bleach: with and without chlorine
  • A cross through the triangle means do not bleach
  • A triangle with diagonal lines means non-chlorine bleach only

Dry Cleaning Symbols

A circle represents dry cleaning.

  • Plain circle means the item can be dry cleaned
  • Letters inside indicate which solvents can be used (A, P, F, W)
  • A cross through the circle means do not dry clean
